◈ PANDORA GATES — LORE
The first gate was discovered after the Fall on Saturn's moon Pandora. Within a year Titanian explorers activated it and traveled beyond the Solar System. Four more gates followed: near Mercury, on Mars, Oberon, and Eris. Hundreds of planets have been discovered — some habitable, some bearing alien life. The Martian Gate at Ma'adim Vallis is under Consortium/Pathfinder control.
THE YETTI GATE The Yetti Gate originates from the Martian Gate at Ma'adim Vallis, operated by Pathfinder. The simians who built this tomb revered the gate as an ancient thing — it had been here for centuries before they found it. They worshipped it. They never managed to activate it.

◈ COMPLICATIONS
C1
Dodecapede Incursion. If the downward tunnel collapsed, surface fissures allowed a number of dodecapedes to enter the Exit Tunnel. They are drawn by the oxygen now leaking through. They will encounter the party if the Exit Tunnel is investigated or used as an escape route.
C2
Parasitoid Sting. One or more PCs may have received a minor suit breach from an apparently-friendly parasitoid. The breach was sealed. Whether this constitutes infection, inoculation, or nothing is a campaign-level decision for the GM. Leave it ambiguous.
C3
Tunnel Collapse Return. Passing back through the unstable section — now further destabilized by the first collapse — risks a complete cave-in that may seal the entire downward passage permanently.
C4
Live Specimen Temptation. The parasitoids are Stage 2: Friends. They are extremely cute. Gatekeeper would pay well for a live specimen. The gate contract almost certainly prohibits unauthorized biological material. And the eggs in the bludgeoned corpses are viable.

◈ GM NOTES — SESSION STRUCTURE
The session's structural pressure is the countdown. Everything the players experience, they experience under a 2-hour clock they cannot negotiate with. The tomb was designed to keep things in, not out — that inversion is the horror. The parasitoids being friendly is the knife: they're cute, they're valuable, and at least one of them has already gotten inside a suit seam.
PACING NOTE The collapse is a choice the players make (pass through the unstable section) with consequences they cannot fully anticipate. Do not rush it. Let the Perceive test happen. Let someone read the ceiling. Then let them decide. The last-person-triggers-collapse rule is the session's best moment — whoever goes last knows what they're signing up for.
The parasitoids should feel like a reward before they feel like a threat. Let the players get attached. Let them name one. The sting moment should almost go unnoticed. That's the whole thing.
