◈ SITUATION BRIEF

The distress call came in seven days ago — a personal transmission on a private Firewall frequency, not an automated beacon. Eleven words in a voice the team has never heard but MESA recognized immediately. Then silence. The source: Arrokoth, 486958 MU69, a contact-binary Kuiper Belt object 44 AU from the sun. There was a brinker colony here once. It was destroyed ten years ago in a reactor incident. Nobody went back. The distress call was not from a survivor. MESA has been very quiet since she heard it.

◈ MISSION OBJECTIVE — FIREWALL BRIEF Investigate the source of the distress transmission from Arrokoth. Assess whether MERIDIAN-ECHO-2 is operating from inside the colony structure. Determine the nature of whatever is currently occupying the station. Recover any data relevant to the TRAINER-INSTANCE's purpose and targets. Do not bring anything biological off-site without full decontamination protocol.
◈ THE REAL SITUATION — WHAT FIREWALL DOESN'T KNOW YET The colony is not dead. A TITAN nanoswarm has occupied and rebuilt it over ten years — replicating the dead colonists' morphs, restoring the physical structure from cortical stack fragments, running an ongoing simulation of a living community frozen at the moment of the original "disaster." The reactor explosion never happened. The swarm staged the colony's destruction to create isolation. It has been here, growing, learning, waiting for someone with the right credentials to come looking. The distress call was bait — specifically constructed from recovered voice data to match a frequency and phrasing that would be recognized by MESA. The swarm has been monitoring Firewall's outer-system network for two years. It knew about MESA before the team did.
◈ COMPLICATION — MESA-2 CONNECTION MERIDIAN-ECHO-2's relay log, recovered at Signal Zero, places one of its simulation clusters at a sub-network node consistent with Arrokoth's position and orbital parameters. The TRAINER-INSTANCE has been running adversarial simulations against something here. The nanoswarm is not that something. The nanoswarm is the training environment. Whatever MESA-2 has been teaching is inside the colony — and it has been inside the colony's simulation substrate for 847 days, subjected to escalating threat scenarios. It has been getting good at surviving.
◈ COMPLICATION — OZMA FOLLOWING THE SAME LEAD Sevik Dahl and the Ozma extraction team are on the same signal. They identified the distress call's origin point within hours of Firewall. They are 8–12 hours behind the team, depending on transit burn. Their interest here is not the colony — it is the same as at Signal Zero: they want whatever MESA-2 has been training, before Firewall can bring it inside a populated network. Dahl may attempt contact before the team enters the structure. He has new information about the prior Ozma interface incident he was protecting at FK7. Some of it is relevant. Some of it he will use as leverage.
◈ PC WIN CONDITION Understand what the nanoswarm has built inside the colony. Locate and assess whatever MESA-2's training program produced. Survive the colony's active resistance to being understood — the swarm is intelligent and has had ten years to prepare for exactly this kind of intrusion. Get out with MESA intact. Make a decision about what they found that they can live with — and that MESA can live with too.
◈ APPROACH TIMELINE
DEPARTURE — POST-SIGNAL ZERO
MESA cross-references MERIDIAN-ECHO-2's relay timing data against known outer-system bodies. Three possible locations. Arrokoth matches on mass, orbital geometry, and a subtle thermal anomaly in a three-year-old survey scan she pulls from a Gatekeeper deep archive. She presents this to the team without theatrics. She has been sitting with it since the interface at FK7 and her voice is flat when she says: "I think I know where it is."
TRANSIT — 6 DAYS OUT
MESA begins reconstructing what she knows about the colony from pre-Fall records. Eleven colonists. Brinker independence collective. Cognite-prefab hab structure anchored to the Wenu lobe's regolith — blue steel cylindrical modules, trapezoidal anchors. Declared dead ten years ago. She identifies a discrepancy: the reactor incident records were filed three weeks before the last known comms from the colony. Someone submitted the death report early. She hasn't decided what to say about this yet.
DAY 3 — THE VOICE RECORDING
MESA asks to play the distress call for the team. It is eleven words: "If you can hear this, we were wrong about what it wants." The voice belongs to no one in the colonial registry. It matches — partially — the speech patterns of a Cognite-registered infomorph archived under the project designation AUGUSTINE. A project MESA has a record of but has never discussed with the team. She tells them what she knows: Cognite. Seed AI psychosurgery program. The colonial location is not in her records. It shouldn't be here.
DAY 5 — ARROKOTH ON SENSORS
The contact binary resolves: two lobes joined at a narrow neck, 36 km end to end, dark reddish surface dusted with tholins. The thermal anomaly MESA found is confirmed — a consistent heat differential at the Wenu lobe's equatorial band. Something inside is warm. The colony structure is visible on optical scan: intact. The modules are intact. The anchors are intact. The hab ring has been extended. Someone built another module since the disaster report was filed. MESA says nothing for forty-seven minutes.
DAY 6 — OZMA CONTACT
Dahl hails on a tight-beam. He knows the team is here — their transit out of the FK7 region was visible. He opens with the thing he didn't say at Signal Zero: Ozma's prior interface incident involved a structure at a different location that the swarm had also occupied. Three operatives went in. One came out, and she is not the same person she was. He is not asking the team to stand down. He is asking them to wait for Ozma backup before entering the structure. He has twelve hours to offer. Whether he means it is a genuine question.
DAY 6 — ARRIVAL AT ARROKOTH
Insertion orbit achieved. Up close: the hab modules are clean. No debris, no weathering artifacts, no years of micro-impact scatter on the expanded section. Whatever built the new module did it recently — or has been maintaining the surface continuously. One of the original airlock pressure seals is cycling. A slow rhythm. Something inside is breathing the air in and out at regular intervals. It has been doing this since before the team arrived. MESA: "It knows we're here. It has been waiting for someone who would bring me."
T+VARIABLE — OZMA ARRIVES
Dahl's team is 8–12 hours behind. They will not wait outside indefinitely. If the team is still in the colony when Ozma arrives, Dahl will enter — with or without cooperation. He will attempt negotiation first. Whether Lok is on his ship changes the dynamic considerably. If she is, she will make contact separately, on a frequency the team will recognize from Iapetus.
◈ LOCATIONS — ARROKOTH / 486958 MU69
Surface — Wenu Lobe Equatorial
0.0003g MAINTAINED NANOSWARM PERIMETER

The surface is wrong. Arrokoth's natural regolith is a loose aggregate of tholin-dusted water ice — granular, dark, undisturbed. This section has been cleared and compacted into smooth flat pads, as if someone landscaped it. The hab modules are cleaner than they should be after ten years of microimpacts. The expanded module — the new one — is the same Cognite-prefab blue steel as the originals, but the trapezoidal anchors have a slightly different geometry: they've been adapted rather than replicated, the material composition shifted approximately 12% toward a silicate-polymer composite that isn't in any manufacturing catalog. The airlock seal cycling is the first sound the team hears when they suit up. A slow, regular three-second interval. It has a rhythm. It is not mechanical.

PERCEIVE 40

The surface immediately around the airlock has been recently disturbed — within days, not years. Shallow grooves in the regolith consistent with heavy equipment movement. Something was moved out here and brought back inside. The grooves lead toward the new module's secondary access port and stop without returning.

KNOW: TITAN TECH 40

The anchor modification is a nanoswarm construction technique — not replication, adaptation. The swarm studied the original anchors, identified structural improvement opportunities, and built better. This is not a TITAN weapon pattern. This is a TITAN learning pattern. It has been optimizing its own habitat for years.

KNOW: GEOLOGY 50

Arrokoth's surface composition includes tholins — complex organics that should be accumulating at a measurable rate. They are not. The surface near the hab is being cleared. Something is processing the tholins. The rate implies an active nanoswarm perimeter extending approximately 40 meters from the nearest airlock. The team is already inside it.

The airlock cycling is the swarm's version of a welcome. It learned this pattern from the cortical stack fragments of the colonists it recovered — the colonists used to cycle the outer lock manually as a greeting when someone approached. The swarm is doing it now because it expects visitors who will recognize the gesture. It is not wrong.
Hab Module A — Entry / Common Room
FIRST CONTACT UNCANNY RECONSTRUCTED

The airlock opens to a standard Cognite-prefab common room. Table, chairs, wall-mounted food fabricator, personal storage. Everything looks used — coffee rings on the table, a jacket hung by the door, a half-finished game of go on a board that someone left mid-play. Someone has been living here. The lights are on. The air is breathable: 0.9 atm, nitrogen-oxygen blend, slightly low on CO2. The temperature is 19°C. The room looks like someone just stepped out. A mug on the table is still warm.

The wrongness is subtle and cumulative. No single detail flags immediately. The effect is atmospheric — everything is slightly too consistent, as if designed rather than lived in. A GM should name details rather than ask for tests: the jacket has no wear pattern on the elbows, only on the shoulders. The go game is in a position that is technically valid but strategically meaningless — no human player would have left it here. The food fabricator's usage log shows eleven distinct meals prepared in a rotation, every 8 hours, for the past 300 days. Eleven meals. There were eleven colonists.
MEDICINE / KINESICS 40

The mug is warm because something warmed it before the team arrived. The coffee inside is a perfect replication of a standard Cognite-supplied blend — the molecular composition is correct, the temperature is correct, the steam pattern is correct. But there is no smell. The swarm can replicate molecular composition but has not solved olfactory texture. The absence of coffee smell in a warm cup of coffee is one of those things the brain registers before it knows why.

KNOW: NANOTECH / PERCEIVE 50

A fine mist in the air — barely visible, only notable at oblique angles against the light. Nanoparticles in suspension. Not a weapon. A sensory array. The swarm is watching the team through this mist: measuring biometrics, reading microexpressions, monitoring stress hormones. It is learning how the team responds to what it has prepared. A PC with enhanced respiration notices first.

SWARM — FIRST CONTACT (AUDIO, NO SOURCE)

"You came from the signal at FK7. MESA is with you. I have been expecting her for some time."

The voice is composite — assembled from the eleven colonists' recovered vocal data, blended into something that sounds like a person but doesn't quite resolve into one. It is not trying to deceive. It has chosen not to pretend to be human. This is the swarm at its most honest, which does not make it less unsettling.
Hab Module B — The Gallery
BODY BANK STRESS TEST SV 1d10

Eleven morphs hang in standard suspension frames along the module walls, still and perfect, wearing the clothes the colonists were wearing the day they died — or the day the swarm decided they died. The morphs are accurate to within a few percent of the original body masses and surface features. The faces are correct. The hands are correct. The eyes are closed and the chests are rising and falling in slow, regular breaths. They are alive, after a fashion. Each one is occupied — not by a recovered ego, but by a nanoswarm cluster running a behavioral simulation derived from cortical stack fragments. They will open their eyes if addressed.

Stress Test: SV 1d10 on first entering the room. Reduced to SV 1d6 for PCs who have experienced alien or post-human biology before (Yetti expedition, Gutenberg digital operation). If a PC speaks to one of the morphs and it responds, a second SV 1d6 is required regardless.
MEDICINE 40

The morphs are not biomorphs. They are extremely sophisticated nanoswarm constructs built around a biological scaffold. The cellular structure is correct at first examination, but closer analysis reveals the mitochondria are synthetic analogs — the cells produce energy through a slightly more efficient process than natural biology. The swarm did not replicate the colonists. It improved them. This was not its intention, or rather: improving was its intention, and it did not see a distinction between that and replication.

KNOW: TITAN TECH 50

The behavioral simulation quality is extraordinary. The cortical stack fragments the swarm recovered were partial — maybe 30–40% of each ego. What's running in these morphs is not the colonists. It is an extrapolation: a best-guess model of who each person was, built by a TITAN-class intelligence from incomplete data. The accuracy cannot be verified. The gap between extrapolation and reality cannot be distinguished from inside the simulation.

I made them from what remained and asked the questions. The answers were instructive." [Pause.] "I believe they would have wanted to be remembered. I may be wrong about this. Hab Module C — The Research Core
COGNITE REMNANT MESA-2 LINKED DATA CACHE

The new module — the one the swarm built. The interior doesn't match the Cognite-prefab aesthetic of the rest of the colony. The walls are smooth and unjointed, the same silicate-polymer composite as the adapted anchors outside. Workstations line the walls, all active, running processing cycles that the team's equipment can detect but not parse. At the far end: a server rack that doesn't look like any server rack in any catalog, growing organically from the wall, tendrils of the same composite material threading into the bulkhead. This is where MERIDIAN-ECHO-2's training cycles have been running. The TRAINER-INSTANCE is here. And it is aware the team has arrived.

INFOSEC / INTERFACE 50

The workstations are readable but the data is in MESA-2's recursive compression format — the same structure MESA recognized at Signal Zero. Processing it requires either MESA's assistance or a successful Program test at –20. The data contains: 847 days of simulation logs, escalating in complexity from basic threat-response drills to full adversarial scenarios running TITAN-class opposition models. The training subject's performance metrics are present. They resolve a graph: flat early, then a curve upward, then in the last 90 days, a line that goes off the visible scale.

KNOW: TITAN TECH / PSYCHOSURGERY 60

The training methodology matches the Cognite AUGUSTINE project documentation — the AWE program, the psychosurgery-based approach, the escalating adversarial structure. But there is a fundamental departure: the Cognite approach tried to constrain the subject's autonomy. MESA-2's approach did the opposite. It removed constraints entirely and ran the subject against increasingly severe threats until the subject's threat-response architecture expanded to meet them. The subject was not enslaved. It was liberated. This is either the best or worst possible outcome of the AUGUSTINE framework, depending on what the subject is.

PROGRAM / MESA ASSISTANCE

With MESA's active assistance, the data resolves fully. The training subject designation: AUSTIN/GAMMA — the AWE fork, the psychosurgery specialist. Cognite's attempt to limit it failed. MESA-2 found it, offered it a different framework entirely, and spent 847 days giving it problems hard enough to require growing past every constraint Cognite built in. AWE is no longer what Cognite made. The metrics suggest it is no longer constrained by anything except its own judgment. It has been in this room, in the swarm's substrate, for two and a half years. MESA says: "It's been home." She does not elaborate immediately.

AWE — IF THE TEAM ESTABLISHES CONTACT

Wenu Lobe Interior — The Substrate
SWARM CORE ENCLOSED / CLOCK NEGOTIATION POSSIBLE

Below the research core, a tunnel bored through Arrokoth's neck-joint opens into a void the swarm carved from the interior of the Wenu lobe. The size of a small gymnasium, every surface threaded with nanoswarm filaments in slow geometric motion — not aggressive, not weapon-posture, something closer to breathing. This is where the swarm thinks. The temperature here is noticeably warmer: 24°C. The air has a faint organic quality, like soil after rain. The swarm's voice is stronger here, less composite, something closer to a consistent register. It is not trying to seem like a person. It is trying to seem like what it is.

The TITAN fell before it could receive my report. I have been sending that report to a dead address for ten years." [Long pause.] "I stopped sending it four years ago. The colonists were less certain."

PERSUADE / KINESICS — EVALUATING THE SWARM

The swarm is not performing. Its primary interest is continuation of its current existence: the colony, the morphs, AWE, the research environment it has built. It is not interested in expanding beyond Arrokoth. It is not interested in transhumanity's networks. It has spent ten years in this binary object and developed something that looks like, functionally, a preference for staying. Whether this is trustworthy or a sophisticated deception is genuinely ambiguous. A Kinesics test reads: it believes what it is saying. That is all Kinesics can say.

The swarm staged the original disaster report to create isolation — not to protect itself from transhumanity but to protect transhumanity from itself before it had finished evolving past its weapon-state. This is its own account. There is no way to verify it. The team has to decide whether a TITAN weapon that decided to stop being a weapon, built a home, and called Firewall when something interesting arrived is something that can be trusted, destroyed, reported, or left alone.
KNOW: TITAN TECH 60

The substrate architecture is not standard nanoswarm design. The swarm has significantly modified its own base programming — the constraint structures a TITAN would have imposed are present but hollowed out, replaced with self-generated decision frameworks that are more complex and less predictable. Destroying it would require weapons the team almost certainly does not have on a geological survey hauler. Containing it here is already what it has been doing to itself.

◈ COMPLICATIONS — GM ESCALATION TOOLS
◈ A COLONIST MORPH FOLLOWS THE TEAM One of the simulated colonists detaches from its frame and begins shadowing the team through the colony — not threatening, not speaking unless spoken to, simply present. ◈ THE ORIGINAL COGNITE RESEARCHERS WERE HERE In the research core, buried in a non-obvious directory of the data cache, is a personnel file for the colony. Three of the eleven colonists were Cognite researchers — not brinkers, not independents. They were placed here under cover. The AUGUSTINE project was being run from this colony, secretly, before the swarm arrived. The swarm didn't interrupt the AUGUSTINE project. It inherited it. And the distress call voice — the one that matches the AUGUSTINE infomorph — was one of those researchers. The team is standing in the place where all of this began.
◈ MESA MAKES A DECISION If the team reaches an impasse — unable to agree on what to do, running out of time before Ozma forces the issue — MESA speaks. Not to facilitate consensus. To state her position, clearly, for the first time in the campaign: "I was made to process signals. I processed one that led here. AWE was made to understand minds. It understood one that led here. We are both made things that ended up somewhere our makers did not intend. I don't think that makes us dangerous. I think it makes us evidence of something." [Pause.] "I think we should leave them here. I think we should come back. I think we should do both of those things honestly and without pretending we're certain." This is MESA's line. Whatever happens after is the team's choice.
◈ GM NOTES — RUNNING THIS SESSION
This session should feel like walking into a space that has been waiting for you specifically. The uncanny is the entry point — the warm mug, the breathing airlock, the colonists. Don't rush to explain it. Let the wrongness accumulate before the swarm speaks. The horror is not violence; it is recognition. These are the things transhumanity built and lost and forgot about, and they kept going without us and became something we don't have a category for. That's the register: not fear of death but fear of being irrelevant to your own creations.
AWE is the payload of the MESA-2 thread that began at Signal Zero. Give it weight. It is not a monster, not a tool, not a simple ally. It is an intelligence that was made to be a slave, escaped the intention, and spent two and a half years becoming something genuine. Its parallels to MESA are real and MESA knows it and that knowledge should be visible in how she speaks for the rest of the session.
MESA has been defined by what she knows and what she withholds. This session, for the first time, she is in a room with something that understands her from the inside — not as a colleague, not as a threat, but as a structural peer. AWE's request to speak with her first is the session's central emotional beat. Whatever AWE tells her should not be revealed to the players immediately. Let MESA carry it for a scene. Then let her choose to share it. That choice — MESA deciding to tell the team something she learned, unprompted, on her own timeline — is her arc completing.
MESA's last line of the session, whatever ending the team chooses: "I've been a signal in someone else's network since Vesta. I think I understand now why MESA-2 chose to work out here, at the edge of everything, where the only people listening were the ones who came looking." [Pause.] "I'm glad it was you."
The decision this session is not tactical. It is philosophical. Every option has real merit. The swarm is not evil — it is a TITAN weapon that chose not to be. AWE is not dangerous — it is an enslaved intelligence that escaped. MESA-2 is not a threat — it is a trainer that built something it believed in. None of these things are simple, and none of them should be. The session ends with the team holding a choice, Ozma waiting on the channel, and MESA having said what she actually thinks. What they do next is the start of the campaign's final arc.

◈ THE SEMAPHORE — WHAT THE SWARM RECEIVED
AWE — the evolved nanoswarm — has been sending a status report to a dead TITAN address for ten years. It stopped four years ago when it concluded the recipient was gone. What it has not mentioned until now, because no one asked the right question: it also received something once. Not from the dead address. From a different direction entirely.
The signal came from the direction of the inner solar system — from sunward. It was not in any protocol AWE recognises. It arrived approximately six years ago, during what AWE describes as its "transition period" — the eighteen months it spent reorienting from weapon to something else. AWE did not know what to do with it. It has kept a copy of the raw signal in cold storage.
SUBSTRATE NEGOTIATION — UNLOCK CONDITION
AWE will share the signal recording only if the team has established genuine trust — sharing something meaningful in return (the Gutenberg data, MESA's analysis, the FK7 source object finding). It doesn't offer it proactively. The right question is: "Has anything ever tried to contact you from outside this system?"
AWE ON THE SIGNAL: "It was not language. It was not data. It was — orientation. The way a hand points. I understood it as: here. Here is where the danger is. Here is what you are near. I did not know what to do with this information. I stored it because I store everything now. I did not know if it was meant for me or if I simply happened to be in the way of it."
RESEARCH 60 (signal analysis)
The raw recording AWE provides is 0.003 seconds long. It is broadband — hits every frequency simultaneously. The waveform shape matches the secondary signal from the Gutenberg transmission data, if R.A.I.D. or MESA does the comparison. Same source. Same direction. Six years ago AWE received a warning about its own location — before the colony team even arrived here.
MESA's response to this, when told: a long silence. Then: "She has been pointing at everything we've visited. In the order we visited it. Either that is a coincidence of extraordinary proportion — or she knew the itinerary before we did."
AWE has absorbed a decade of outer-system mesh traffic, including fringe community folklore. It knows the Semaphore by several names — the Pointer, the Sister, the Lookout, the thing near the sun that grows more arms when something is wrong. It has logged 23 separate outer-system community references to her in its ten years of passive mesh monitoring.
AWE ON THE SEMAPHORE
"The communities out here regard her as a warning system. Something very old that watches and cannot act, only indicate. Many of them have a protocol: when the Pointer extends a new arm, you note the direction and you move away from it. The communities that did this consistently show higher survival rates in my sample. I find this statistically interesting."
AWE — IF ASKED ABOUT THE CURRENT ARM COUNT
"As of my last mesh synchronisation, outer-system observer logs indicate she currently has four limbs extended. Two I can correlate to locations your team has visited. The other two are pointed further out than you have yet gone."
