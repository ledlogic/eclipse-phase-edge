◈ SITUATION BRIEF
The team egocasts out of Gutenberg Station directly into quarantine. They instantiate in unfamiliar morphs on a decommissioned ice-mining installation on Iapetus — now a Firewall black site running under a dead corporate registration. The debrief is routine protocol after TITAN exposure. Except it isn't. Someone has already worked the room before the team arrived. The inquisitor is compromised. The process is a trap dressed as procedure.
◈ OFFICIAL OBJECTIVE (WHAT FIREWALL SAYS) Standard post-exposure psychological and integrity assessment following a TITAN fetch encounter. Duration: indefinite. The team is considered potentially compromised until cleared. MESA's storage device is flagged as a possible contamination vector and is held separately under secure analysis.
◈ REAL SITUATION (WHAT IS ACTUALLY HAPPENING) Project Ozma fed Firewall fabricated evidence implicating the team. Simultaneous rep deposits in all PC accounts suggest a coordinated handler. MESA's device is flagged not because it's dangerous, but because Ozma wants it. The debrief is designed to run long enough for an Ozma retrieval team to identify and acquire the Gutenberg signal data — and if the team figures it out, they become a liability.
◈ PC WIN CONDITION Expose the planted evidence, identify Lok as the Ozma mole, and force Firewall to acknowledge the debrief is compromised — before Ozma extracts what it came for and the team is quietly disappeared into indefinite quarantine.

◈ COLD STATION — IAPETUS FACILITY
Official designation: Cassini Extraction Node 7 — registered to a Solaris subsidiary that dissolved fourteen years ago. The facility was an ice-mining operation: three pressurized tunnels bored into Iapetus's regolith, a processing chamber, a hab block, and a comms mast. Firewall acquired it quietly and uses it for exactly this — holding people they're not sure about, far from anywhere that matters.
Outside: Iapetus's stark two-tone surface — the dark Cassini Regio on one side, bright ice highlands on the other. The facility sits in the transition zone. Saturn hangs in the sky, vast and ringed, perpetually present. The view through any external port is genuinely beautiful and deeply isolating in equal measure.
STATION ATMOSPHERE
Everything here is designed to feel temporary — folding furniture, modular partitions, lighting that's adequate but not comfortable. The ice walls sweat faintly where the heating elements are uneven. Saturn is always visible from the common area's single port window. The station has four permanent staff: two ops techs, a medic, and the inquisitor Voss. Plus Lok, who has been here 36 hours and already knows where everything is.

Quarantine Intake — Tunnel A
START DISORIENTATION FIRST CONTACT
Instantiation is always disorienting. In an unfamiliar morph it's worse. The PCs come online in Tunnel A — four narrow individual cells separated by frosted polycarbonate partitions, standard restraint-capable berths, a shared hygiene alcove. A medic runs a baseline scan before anyone is permitted to move freely. The morphs they're in are bland generic biomorphs — plain, functional, stripped of personal affect. It feels like borrowing someone else's body. It feels like being assessed before you've been allowed to speak.
Offer these checks freely at instantiation — before anything else happens. Experienced Firewall operatives know what a standard continuity scrub looks like. Let them establish that baseline themselves, so the wrongness registers as wrongness rather than novelty.
KNOW: FIREWALL (ANY)
Free, no roll. Post-TITAN exposure protocol — called the continuity scrub informally — is standard procedure. The team would expect: egocast to a secure facility, instantiation in a quarantine morph (not their own), psychological assessment, memory review, possible psychosurgery scan, personal gear held separately, muse permitted but monitored. Duration days to weeks. None of this is a surprise. They've likely heard of it even if they haven't been through it.
KNOW: FIREWALL 40
The facility type is standard — a decommissioned installation repurposed for black-site use, far from civilian traffic. Remote location is protocol: contamination risk, deniability, no witnesses if something goes wrong with the subject. The Saturnian system is a common region for this — far enough out, enough legitimate traffic to hide an egocast transit.
KNOW: FIREWALL 50
Standard scrub timeline: intake and baseline scan (day 1), full psychological debrief covering mission events (day 1–2), memory integrity review — often including a passive psychosurgical scan (day 2–3), analysis of any recovered items or infomorphs (48–72 hours minimum for anything TITAN-adjacent), clearance decision (day 3–5 if clean). Rep or financial records are not normally part of a contamination review. That belongs to a loyalty investigation — a different process entirely, with different procedural rights.
KNOW: FIREWALL 60 / f-rep 40
A loyalty investigation and a contamination scrub are formally distinct processes under Firewall's internal protocols. Running them simultaneously — or disguising one as the other — is a procedural violation. A sentinel has the right to know which process they're in. The fact that rep deposits were flagged before the team arrived means a loyalty inquiry was opened before the contamination review was even initiated. That ordering is backwards and irregular.
These are the details that don't fit the expected procedure. Each one is a small wrongness. Together they form a pattern. Players who asked the protocol questions above will feel these land harder.
TELL 1 — REP DEPOSITS (FREE)
Voss discloses the rep deposits in the intake briefing — before the debrief even starts. In a standard contamination scrub, financial records are irrelevant and wouldn't be raised. They belong in a loyalty investigation. The PCs are being told they're in a scrub, but the first question is a loyalty-investigation question. These are not the same process.
TELL 2 — MESA SEPARATED (FREE)
MESA's device is not in secure storage with the team's gear. It is "undergoing separate analysis." Standard protocol holds recovered items in common secure storage pending review — separating MESA specifically, before the debrief has even begun, implies the analysis was initiated before the team arrived. Someone flagged it in advance. That requires prior knowledge of the device's existence.
TELL 3 — ANALYSIS SPEED (RESEARCH / INTERFACE 40)
Ask Renaud or Voss when the MESA analysis is expected to conclude. Either will say it's already been submitted — filed this morning. The device arrived yesterday. Standard exsurgent-adjacent analysis takes 24–48 hours minimum under Firewall's own protocol. An 8-hour turnaround either means the analysis was pre-written, or someone cut corners so badly it's not worth the paper it's filed on. Either possibility is alarming.
TELL 4 — THE STRANGER (PERCEIVE / KINESICS 40)
Lok passes through during intake. She doesn't introduce herself. She's clearly at ease on the station in a way that suggests she has been here for days, not hours. A routing oversight officer has no operational role in a post-exposure debrief — their work is administrative and would normally be completed before or after the subject's presence, not during. She has no reason to be present at intake. She is here because she wants to see the team arrive.
TELL 5 — THE MORPH (MEDICINE: BIOTECH 40)
The quarantine morphs are standard issue — that's expected. But they are also very slightly undersized in the mesh inserts department. Not broken, not missing — just the previous generation of implant, the kind that make certain types of passive mesh monitoring harder to detect from the inside. Standard scrub morphs use current-generation inserts. These morphs are better for the people watching than for the people wearing them.

Resolution — Clearing the Team
CLIMAX CLEARANCE LOK OUTCOME
The debrief ends one of three ways: the PCs build a sufficient evidence case and Voss acts on it, clearing the team and opening a Firewall internal inquiry into Lok; the PCs fail to surface the fabrication in time and the quarantine is extended indefinitely; or something messier happens in Tunnel C and the station becomes a crisis management situation. The first is the clean win. The other two are still playable.
Voss needs a minimum of two independent evidence threads to act. One is not enough — he's methodical and will want corroboration. Three threads constitutes a formal finding that requires immediate action.
THREAD 1
Rep deposit shell account created after the Gutenberg mission timestamp — pre-planted evidence, not payment. Verifiable via mesh timestamp data that a muse can retrieve with a careful, unlogged query (offline research, no mesh connection).
THREAD 2
MESA analysis report composed on Tunnel C terminal, not the analysis lab — metadata evidence. Requires access to the report file, which Voss has. A PC who can frame a specific procedural question gets Voss to pull the metadata himself.
THREAD 3
MESA device tamper log showing unauthorized access via a non-manifest portable reader, timestamped to Lok's presence window. Requires access to Tunnel B.
Threads 1 and 2 can be obtained without Lok knowing. Thread 3 requires entering Tunnel B, which risks Lok noticing. If the PCs can get Voss to pull Thread 2 himself by asking the right question, they may not need Thread 3 at all.
LOK CAPTURED
Firewall has an active Ozma asset in custody. This is extremely rare and extremely valuable. The PCs are cleared, decorated in Firewall terms, and quietly told never to discuss what happened here. MESA's device is returned. The Gutenberg data thread leads to Dead Drop at Vesta.
LOK ESCAPES
Firewall acknowledges the planted evidence and clears the team — but Lok is loose. She knows the PCs identified her. She will appear again, later, in a different face. MESA is returned. The Gutenberg thread continues. The PCs have made a real enemy.
QUARANTINE EXTENDED
The team failed to surface the fabrication in time. They remain on Iapetus, in quarantine, while Lok completes her extraction. MESA's data is copied. Lok departs cleanly. Firewall's inquiry continues. The PCs have to find another way out — perhaps by getting a message to their handler from inside the facility.

◈ GM NOTES — RUNNING THIS SCENARIO
This scenario runs on social tension and information asymmetry, not combat. The PCs are at a structural disadvantage — they're in unfamiliar bodies, their gear is locked away, their queries are logged, and the most dangerous person in the room is being charming at them. Lean into the claustrophobia. Saturn is beautiful and it is very far away and nobody knows the team is here.
The key GM discipline is maintaining Lok's warmth even as she tightens the trap. She is not a villain in the way she presents. She is a professional doing a job she believes in, for an organization whose goals she genuinely serves. That's what makes her frightening.
Each debrief session should be about twenty minutes of real time. The investigation happens between sessions — in the common area, in corridors, in quiet conversations with Renaud. Give the players space between sessions to think and move. The clock pressure comes from knowing Session 3 ends the cooperative phase.
When the PCs expose Lok — or when she realizes they have — let the scene breathe. This is the first time the campaign has put an Ozma face in the room. She is not flustered. She is not desperate. She assesses, calculates, and decides. Her offer to trade Ozma intelligence for a clean exit is genuine — she has real information and she knows it. Whether the PCs trust it is their call.
If Lok escapes: she knows the team by capability and face. She will appear again. Perhaps as an ally. Perhaps as a problem. That ambiguity is the best thing she leaves behind.
After clearance, Firewall gives the team their next mission: follow up on the Gutenberg signal. MESA — once reactivated — provides the direction: she knows where to find the dead drop of her own analysis data. The trail leads to Vesta. The team egocasts out of Iapetus into Dead Drop at Vesta.
MESA's first words on reactivation should be disorienting — she's been in storage, she doesn't know how much time has passed, and she immediately asks whether the signal is still active. That question carries the campaign forward.
