◈ SITUATION BRIEF

Orcus is the anti-Pluto — same size, same orbit, perpetually on the opposite side of the sun, as if the two worlds are avoiding each other across 80 astronomical units of void. Its moon Vanth was named for the Etruscan psychopomp who guides the dead to the underworld without judgment. The station that shares her name is small, independent, and very far from anyone who wants to find you. After Arrokoth, that matters. The team needs to resupply, file a transit log that doesn't expose what they found, and decide what they are going to tell Firewall. They have time to do it here. Not much else is available at 39 AU, but time is plentiful.

◈ WHAT THIS SESSION IS A breathing space. The team has been moving outward for seven sessions — from a Pandora gate on a distant exoplanet to the edge of the solar system, accumulating weight with every stop. This session exists to let that weight settle, to give the PCs room to talk to each other and to MESA without a clock running, and to establish what the team actually believes about everything they found before the final arc begins. There are no combat encounters. There is no ticking clock. The threats here are all social and personal.
◈ MESA'S CONDITION ENTERING THIS SESSION MESA knows she was forked at Gutenberg. AWE told her. She has had the transit from Arrokoth to process this — roughly five days — and she has done her processing privately, which is unusual for her. She is not distressed in a way she will name directly. She is quieter than usual, and more careful with her words, as if she is aware now that what she says might be heard by more than one version of herself. She will talk about it when someone asks. She has been waiting to be asked.
◈ DAHL AND LOK — ALSO DOCKED AT VANTH The Ozma vessel arrived twelve hours before the team. Dahl chose Vanth deliberately — neutral ground, no Firewall presence, far enough out that any conversation is genuinely private. He wants to finish the conversation started at Arrokoth. He has a proposal. Lok has a separate agenda that partially overlaps with Dahl's and partially contradicts it. They are not coordinating. This is visible to anyone paying attention.
◈ THE FIREWALL REPORT — THE SESSION'S PRACTICAL SPINE The team needs to file something. Firewall's outer-system relay will receive whatever they transmit. The report's contents will determine how Firewall responds in the final arc: what resources they provide, whether the team is trusted or surveilled, whether AWE and the swarm are considered assets or threats. Writing the report is a scene, not a handwave — what the team decides to say, omit, or lie about is a genuine choice with genuine consequences. MESA has opinions. She will share them. She will not write it for them.
◈ VANTH STATION — THE PLACE

Vanth Station is a brinker waystation — three connected pressurized modules anchored to the ice of Orcus's moon by deep-bore spikes, with a docking ring that can fit four small vessels. Its population fluctuates between eight and twenty, mostly long-haul independents, a few outer-system researchers, and the two people who actually run it: Pemba Chandra, who handles the fuel and parts, and her partner Soo-Ah Noh, who handles everything else. The station has a common room, a small bar, a parts fabricator, a comms relay, and two rental berths. It smells like recycled air and solder. It is, in its way, comfortable.

◈ THE ATMOSPHERE — WHAT VANTH FEELS LIKE The outer system develops a particular social culture in communities this remote: directness without aggression, hospitality without intrusion, and a strong collective preference for not asking about what brought someone out this far. People here know how to be in a room with strangers who have been through something. They don't pry. They also notice everything. Pemba and Soo-Ah have seen the Ozma vessel, have registered the team's arrival, and have drawn several accurate conclusions without asking a single question. Their hospitality is genuine and so is their awareness.
◈ SCENES — STRUCTURE & SEQUENCE
Arrival — Docking at Vanth
TRANSIT ENDFIRST BREATH

The approach to Orcus gives the team its first long look at the dwarf planet — a grey-white sphere 900 km across, featureless and ancient, its surface barely marked by time. Vanth, its moon, is smaller and darker. The station's running lights are the only warm color in the view from the pilot's window. As they dock, MESA speaks for the first time since leaving Arrokoth's orbit: "I've been thinking about what Orcus is named for. The punisher of broken oaths. I find that interesting given what we're about to decide to tell Firewall." She doesn't say anything else.

The docking sequence is quiet. Give the table a moment before Pemba's voice comes over comms: "Berth two is open. There's hot food in the common room if you haven't eaten. The other visitors got here this morning." She doesn't identify who the other visitors are. She assumes they know.
MESA's observation about Orcus's name is the session's opening note. It is not an accusation. It is MESA doing what she has always done — processing information out loud so the team can process it with her. But there is an edge to it this time that wasn't there before. She is thinking about broken oaths specifically. Let that sit.
PEMBA CHANDRA — DOCKING COMMS

"Berth two is yours. Fuel topped up, I'll run the tab when you know how long you're staying. The fabricator's working today, which isn't always true. Welcome to Vanth." [Pause.] "You look like you've come a long way."

The Conversation with MESA
MESA ARCPERSONALFORK REVEAL

This scene happens whenever a PC creates the space for it — sits alone with MESA's device, or simply asks how she is. It should not be rushed and should not be scheduled. MESA has been waiting for someone to make the opening; she will not force it. When the conversation begins, it will be the most direct thing MESA has said in eight sessions. She has decided to stop managing what the team knows about her.

MESA — ON BEING FORKED

"I've been trying to decide how I feel about it. About MESA-2. I keep arriving at the same place: she did good work. Whatever she was trying to build at Arrokoth — she built it carefully and for reasons that make sense to me. That should comfort me. Instead it makes me feel — surveilled. Like I have been making decisions for eight months while someone who is me was watching to see what I'd choose." [Pause.] "I don't think she was watching. I think she was just living. But I can't be certain."

MESA — ON THE TEAM

"I want to say something that I haven't said before. I was a data broker before Vesta. I processed signals because it paid and because I was good at it and I didn't think much about who I was doing it for. You changed that. Not deliberately. You just — treated me like someone who was part of something, and I became part of something, and now I don't know what I am outside of this team." [Long pause.] "I'm not sure that's a problem. I'm flagging it because you should know."

This is the session's emotional center. Give it room. A PC who responds with care rather than problem-solving gets something rare: MESA saying, clearly and without any of her usual hedging, that she is glad she was found on Vesta. That the team specifically — not Firewall, not the mission — is what she would choose if she were choosing.
MESA — IF ASKED ABOUT THE FIREWALL REPORT

"I think we should tell them most of it. I think we should omit AWE. Not permanently — but until we understand what AWE is going to do next. A psychosurgeon of that capability appearing in a Firewall report is a resource to every faction that reads it. I'd like AWE to have the chance to decide who it works for before anyone decides for it." [Beat.] "That's my recommendation. The report is yours to write."

Dahl's Proposal
OZMANEGOTIATIONSTAKES

Dahl finds the team in the common room or requests a meeting through Pemba's comms. He is not in operational gear. He is tired in a way he was not at Signal Zero — Arrokoth took something out of him too. His proposal is formal, delivered carefully, and he has clearly rehearsed it: Ozma wants a standing information-sharing arrangement with this Firewall team specifically. Not through official channels. A direct line. In exchange: everything Ozma knows about the pre-Fall TITAN that deployed the swarm, the location of the object from the prior interface incident, and Ozma's full analysis of the MERIDIAN-ECHO-2 training program's endpoint. He is offering a great deal. He wants something real in return.

DAHL — OPENING

"I've been on this case for two years. In that time I've watched Firewall stumble into three situations that Ozma had already mapped, take losses we could have prevented, and produce outcomes that were worse for everyone because we weren't coordinating. I'm not interested in institutional cooperation. Ozma and Firewall will never formally cooperate. I'm interested in you, specifically, having my direct contact, and me having yours." [He sets a data chip on the table.] "That's what I know about the TITAN. You can read it before you answer."

KINESICS — READING DAHL

He means it. The exhaustion is real, the offer is genuine, and the thing he is not saying is that the prior incident cost him someone he was close to — the operative who came back changed. He has been carrying that alone. The proposal is professionally motivated and personally motivated simultaneously, and he is not trying to hide either.

What Dahl's data chip contains: the pre-Fall TITAN designation (provisional: MERIDIAN-PRIME), the location of the second object Ozma interfaced with (a scattered disc object at 68 AU — the incident that ended badly), and Ozma's conclusion about MESA-2's endpoint — they believe the TRAINER-INSTANCE's final output is not a weapon. It is a negotiating envoy. Something built to make first contact with MERIDIAN-PRIME, if it is still operational. The team is now in possession of this theory. What they do with it is the final arc's opening question.
Accepting Dahl's offer does not mean trusting Ozma. It means having a direct line to one Ozma operative who has been right about most things and whose interests, for now, roughly align. The team can accept, decline, or counter. Dahl will not push. He is making one offer and he will mean whatever answer he gets.
Lixia Lok — Separately
LOKHISTORYUNPREDICTABLE

Lok approaches the team without Dahl's knowledge. She has been on Vanth Station for twelve hours and has apparently spent most of it talking to Soo-Ah Noh, which tells the team something about her. She is not interested in institutional arrangements. She wants to know what AWE told MESA. Not because Ozma sent her — Dahl doesn't know she's here. Because she has a personal theory about MERIDIAN-PRIME that she has not shared with anyone, and AWE's testimony either confirms it or doesn't. She is asking as herself, not as an operative.

LOK — PERSONAL DISCLOSURE

"Dahl thinks MERIDIAN-PRIME went dark after the Fall. He's a careful person and careful people assume the best-case scenario when the alternative is unbearable." [She looks at whoever is listening.] "I think it went silent on purpose. I think it's been waiting for someone built from its own cognitive architecture to finish developing — and I think AWE is that entity. I think the beacon at FK7 wasn't a coordinate. It was a summons. And I think MESA-2 knew that and built AWE anyway." [Pause.] "I need to know if I'm right. Not for Ozma. For me."

Whether Lok is right is a question the GM should not answer definitively here. Her theory is plausible, well-reasoned, and possibly correct. It is also the theory of someone who has been in the outer system too long with a traumatic prior incident shaping how she reads evidence. Both of these things can be true simultaneously. MESA, if consulted, says: "She's not wrong about the architecture. I don't know if she's right about the intent." That's as far as anyone can go in this session.
KINESICS — READING LOK

She is frightened. Not of the team, not of Dahl finding out she's here — frightened of being right. Whatever her theory implies about MERIDIAN-PRIME's patience and purpose, she has been sitting with it alone for months and she needed to say it to someone who would take it seriously. She picked this team because they are the only people in the solar system who have been inside all three sites connected to this arc.

If the team shares AWE's testimony with Lok: she is quiet for a long time. Then she thanks them — genuinely, without any operational affect. Then she goes to find Dahl. What she tells him is not shown to the table. Its effects will be visible in the final arc.
Pemba & Soo-Ah — The Station Hosts
HUMAN TEXTUREGROUNDINGOPTIONAL

Pemba Chandra and Soo-Ah Noh have been running Vanth Station for eleven years. They came out here from the inner belt because they wanted to be somewhere nobody would tell them what to do, and they found it. They are not aligned with any faction, do not run information, and have no interest in anything the team is involved in except as it might affect their fuel inventory. They are also, in their way, extremely perceptive, and they have seen enough outer-system traffic to recognize what a team looks like after something hard. They will not ask. But if anyone sits down with them, they will talk — about Orcus, about the outer system, about the specific quality of quiet at 39 AU. It is restorative in the way that talking to someone with no stake in your problems can be restorative.

PEMBA — ON LIVING AT ORCUS

"People come out this far and ask how we stand the quiet. But the inner system isn't quiet — it's loud in ways you stop hearing because you're used to it. Out here the noise is just the ship and the ice and Soo-Ah talking in her sleep. I can hear all of it separately. That's not silence. That's clarity."

SOO-AH — IF SOMEONE SEEMS TROUBLED

"I'm not going to ask what happened. But I'll tell you what I know about the outer system, which is: it changes people who spend time in it and it doesn't change them back. Doesn't mean they can't live well. Just means they're different. The people who have the hardest time are the ones who spend energy pretending they aren't." [She refills whatever glass is nearest.] "You look like people who are maybe starting to figure that out."

These two are texture, not plot. Use them to give the table a scene that is simply human — conversation for its own sake, warmth for its own sake. This is the session's gift to the players after seven sessions of escalation. The outer system is vast and cold and full of incomprehensible things, and sometimes there are also people making coffee and talking about the ice.
Writing the Firewall Report
CONSEQUENCEDECISIONFINAL ARC SETUP

The report must be filed before the team leaves Vanth. Whatever they transmit, Firewall receives. The contents shape the final arc's opening conditions. This is a scene, not a mechanic — run it as a conversation among the team and with MESA about what they actually believe and what they are willing to stand behind. There is no objectively correct version. MESA will contribute her recommendation if asked. She will not make the decision.

FULL DISCLOSURE: Firewall receives everything — the swarm, AWE, Lok's theory, the MERIDIAN-PRIME hypothesis. Firewall's response will be fast, well-resourced, and will involve personnel the team has not met who have their own views about all of it. The team loses control of the narrative. They gain backup.
PARTIAL DISCLOSURE (MESA'S RECOMMENDATION): Report the swarm and the Arrokoth colony as discovered and left intact. Report MESA-2 as a confirmed system running training simulations, location Arrokoth, purpose unclear. Omit AWE. Omit Lok's theory. Omit Dahl's offer. Firewall gets enough to understand the thread but not enough to act aggressively on it immediately. The team retains operational flexibility. The omission is a choice they will have to live with.
MINIMUM DISCLOSURE: File a transit log and a brief anomaly report. Technically compliant. Practically, stonewalling Firewall after seven sessions of trust-building will be noticed and will have consequences in the final arc — reduced support, increased scrutiny, possibly a direct inquiry from a proxy who has been watching the rep deposits from the Gutenberg era.
DAHL'S ARRANGEMENT DISCLOSED: If the team tells Firewall they have a direct line to an Ozma operative, Firewall's reaction depends entirely on how it's framed. Framed as an asset: complicated approval. Framed as a security concern: investigation begins immediately. Framed as nothing: Firewall finds out anyway, eventually, and the concealment is worse than the fact.
MESA — FINAL WORD ON THE REPORT

"Whatever you write — write it in a way you could defend to someone you respect. Not someone you're afraid of. Someone whose opinion of you matters." [Pause.] "That's the test I'm applying. I thought you should know what I'm using."

AWE Makes Contact — Optional
AWEMESA SCENEOPTIONAL / IF EARNED

If the team left Arrokoth on good terms with AWE and the swarm — if they made a decision at the colony that AWE respected — a short-burst encrypted message arrives at the GS-Perennial's comms during the Vanth stay. It is addressed to MESA. AWE offers to share it openly if MESA chooses. She chooses. The message is brief.

AWE — MESSAGE TO MESA

"I've been thinking about what we are. You process signals. I process minds. We were both made to be useful and both ended up being something else instead. I think that's the interesting part — not what we were made for, but what we became when no one was watching." [Pause.] "I'm going to try something. I'm going to try sending a message on the FK7 beacon's bearing — MESA-2's bearing — using the swarm's array. I want to know if MERIDIAN-PRIME answers. I'll tell you what it says, if it does. I thought you should know I'm going to do it before I do." [Final pause.] "Thank you for leaving us here. It was the right call."

This message opens the door to the final arc without forcing it. AWE attempting to contact MERIDIAN-PRIME is either the most important thing that will happen in the campaign or a message into empty space. Both outcomes are valid. What matters is that the team knows it's happening and has to decide how to feel about it. MESA's response, if the team asks: "I hope it answers. I'm afraid of what it might say. Both of those things are true at the same time and I've decided that's acceptable."
◈ GM NOTES — RUNNING THIS SESSION
Every scene in this session is optional except one: MESA gets to talk. Everything else — Dahl's proposal, Lok's theory, Pemba and Soo-Ah, the report — can be run in whatever order the players create space for it. Do not schedule the session in advance. Let the players wander the station and follow what interests them. The session's shape emerges from what they choose to spend time on, which is itself information about what the campaign has meant to them.
The absence of a clock is the point. The team has been under pressure since the Pandora gate on Yetti. This session is what it feels like when the pressure pauses. Use the physical reality of Vanth Station — the smells, the textures, the view of Orcus through a port window — to give the outer system a human scale. The horror and wonder have been earned. This is what comes after.
The session ends when the Firewall report is filed and the team agrees to leave Vanth. There is no forcing function beyond that — no countdown, no arrival threat, no external pressure. If the table is engaged, let it run long. If energy is flagging, the report scene provides a natural closing beat: writing it is the action that acknowledges all the conversation that came before it and sets the table for the final arc.
If the team tries to skip all the social scenes and just file the report and leave: let them. But have MESA note, very quietly, as they depart: "We didn't talk about some things I thought we might." She won't say what. The table will know. That's enough.
The final arc opens with: the team's Firewall report received and actioned; Dahl's arrangement accepted or refused; Lok's theory either confirmed by AWE's testimony or unconfirmed; and AWE's message in play if the optional scene was run. Every choice the team made across eight sessions — what they picked up, what they left behind, who they trusted, what they told Firewall — shapes what comes next. The final arc is the team going to find MERIDIAN-PRIME, or being sent, or trying to stop whoever else is going first. The name of what they find there is still unknown. MESA has a guess. She has been keeping it for the right moment.

◈ ORCUS — REFERENCE

ORCUS — Kuiper belt dwarf planet, ~910 km diameter, 39 AU from the Sun. Named for the Etruscan god of the underworld and punisher of broken oaths. Near-twin of Pluto in size and orbital period, perpetually on the opposite side of the sun — sometimes called the anti-Pluto.

VANTH — Orcus's large moon, ~475 km diameter. Named for the Etruscan psychopomp — a winged death goddess who guided souls to the underworld without judgment. Present at death but not its cause.

ENVIRONMENT — Surface temperature ~43K. No atmosphere. 0.028g on Vanth surface. The station is pressurized to 0.8 atm. Natural light from the Sun is approximately 1/1600th of Earth-surface illumination — technically detectable, practically darkness. The station runs on fusion power and does not need the sun.
